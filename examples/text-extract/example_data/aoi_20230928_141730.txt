ARTICLE I: INCORPORATION

This Articles of Incorporation is hereby established on this 14th day of November, 2022, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the State Corporate Law. The name of this corporation is "New Horizons Innovations Inc."

ARTICLE II: REGISTERED OFFICE

The principal office for transactions is located at 7823 Serenity Lane, Barkley, Pennsylvania. The name of the agent for service of process at such address is James H. Barnett.

ARTICLE III: PURPOSE 

This corporation is organized for the purpose of engaging in any lawful act or activity for which corporations may be organized under the General Corporation Law of Pennsylvania except as otherwise limited in this article. Specifically, the corporation will focus on advanced software designs, product development and integrated technology services.

ARTICLE IV: SHARE CAPITAL

The corporation is authorized to issue two Hundred Thousand (200,000) shares; all of which are to be of one class and to have a par value of $1 dollar per share.

ARTICLE V: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

The Business and affairs shall be managed, and all corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the direction of a Board of Directors composed of six (6) members.

ARTICLE VI: INDEMNIFICATION OF DIRECTORS AND OFFICERS

The corporation may indemnify any person who was or is a party to any proceeding by reason of the fact that he/she is or was a director/officer against expenses incurred in relation to the proceedings, in the manner and to the extent permitted by the Corporation Law.

ARTICLE VII: LIMITATION OF DIRECTOR LIABILITY

No director of the corporation shall be personally liable to the corporation or its shareholders for damages for breach of fiduciary duty, except for liability of director for (1) acts of omissions which involve intentional malfeasance or a knowing violation of law, (2) unlawful corporate distributions, or (3) any transaction from which the director derived improper personal benefit.

This Articles of Incorporation of New Horizons Innovations Inc. revoke any and all provisions inconsistent with the foregoing provisions.
