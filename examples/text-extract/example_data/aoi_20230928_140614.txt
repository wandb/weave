## ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION 

OF

## FAIR PIONEER TECHNOLOGIES, INC.


Pursuant to the provisions of the General Corporation Law of the State of Rhode Island, USA;

The undesignated hereby adopts the following Articles of Incorporation:

---

ARTICLE 1 - NAME:

The name of the Corporation is Fair Pioneer Technologies, Inc.

---

ARTICLE 2 - PRINCIPAL OFFICE:

The address of the Corporation’s initial principal office is 1430 Radcliffe Street, Providence, RI 02903

---

ARTICLE 3 - REGISTERED AGENT:

The name and address of the Corporation’s initial Registered Agent is Davidson Price, 259 Columbia Avenue, Cranston, RI 02905. 

---

ARTICLE 4 - PURPOSE:

The purpose of the Corporation is to engage in any lawful act or activity for which corporations may be organized under the General Corporation Law of Rhode Island. 

The Corporation is organized to operate as a technology product and service provider, including software development, cybersecurity consultancy, and digital solutions provision.

---

ARTICLE 5 - SHARE CAPITAL:

The total number of shares that the Corporation is authorized to issue is 510,000 shares, divided into two classes: 

- 500,000 shares of Common Stock, no par value per share.
- 10,000 shares of Preferred Stock, no par value per share.

---

ARTICLE 6 - INCORPORATORS:

The name and address of each incorporator are as below:

- Sarah Williamson, 326 Eastwood Ave, Providence, RI 02905
- Alice Stevenson, 541 Warwick Street, Woonsocket, RI 02895

---

ARTICLE 7 - BOARD OF DIRECTORS:

The number of directors constituting the initial board of directors is two. The names and addresses of the persons who are to serve as directors are the incorporators listed herein. 

---

ARTICLE 8 - INDEMNIFICATION:

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the corporation is authorized to provide indemnification of (and advancement of expenses to) directors, officers and agents of the corporation (and any other persons to which General Corporation Law permits the corporation to provide indemnification).

---

ARTICLE 9 - LIABILITY:

To the fullest extent permitted by the General Corporation Law of the State of Rhode Island, none of our directors will be personally liable to us or our shareholders for monetary damages for breach for policy duty as a director.
